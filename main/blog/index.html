Baby's first blog

3 month update

So, in response to Rob's increasingly passive-aggressive digs at me about how this was supposed to be 'my' blog I've finally managed to put Bertie down for a nap (it's only taken 11 weeks to do so) - and he's woken up again, got to go... 

10 minutes later...

Right, time to try again. There were 2 main reasons we set up this website. 1) Rob needed a project. There's a lot written about how blokes don't really get on board with the whole baby thing until it's out as it's not real for them yet whereas for women, well, we can't really avoid it! Awake again... 

20 minutes later...

So Rob was looking for a reason to beef up his coding skills and I was looking for a way to make him feel part of the process so setting up a website for photos and information about the little one seemed like a good idea. 2) I really didn't want photos on Facebook. It seems a somewhat silly line to draw, I'm happy to have them on the wider internet, just not on social media but it's one that makes sense to me. As kids get older they get so much information about data security, how to keep yourself safe online etc etc. But their parents aren't following any of these guidelines they've been dishing out. It seems somewhat hypocritical to tell a 13 year old that they have to be careful of their online identity when they've already got 100s of photos out there online that they don't even know about. So setting up a website that’s not indexed by google that is only accessible to those I chose to tell about it is a nice way to show family and friends how he's doing without spamming my Facebook account. 

I did not enjoy being pregnant at all. I'm super happy with my little boy and would not go back and change things (aside from less sickness, better skin etc etc.) but I'm glad the whole thing is over until next time – if there is one! The first week in hospital was overwhelming and although if you'd asked me before the birth about spending time in hospital I would have said it would be worst case scenario, actually I think it was the best thing that could have happened. I got so much support from the midwives about everything but especially breastfeeding that it was really beneficial. It did make coming home that bit scarier though – I was used to a professional telling me every 6 hours that Bertie was healthy and now it was all on us! 

Amazon Prime is hands down the best thing we've bought for the baby. Anytime I have a panic or we think of something that would be handy it can be there in 24 hours. Getting it delivered to the actual door rather than just the manager's office/going to collect it from the gate on the other hand is substantially trickier. In the first few weeks I almost had a meltdown with how much stuff we had in the flat, how was it all going to fit, where was it going to go but now it just feels normal. My need to organise everything to within an inch of its life has come in pretty handy for fitting things into nooks and crannies around the flat and creating some semblance of order. 

It feels somewhat surreal to be honest. About 2 years after we got married I was still occasionally thinking 'oh, I have to organise that wedding' and then remembering we'd already done that! This feels the same, Bertie's here and I'm still sometimes thinking about what we need to do when we have a baby! My style of parenting can be described as winging it with a liberal dose of Google to find viewpoints that justify what you were going to do anyway.  We had a whole 20 minutes of sleep there, we're blessed... 

27 hours later...

Some things have been easier than I thought, some things harder and others about right. The day to day physical looking after him is dead easy – I can change a nappy in under a minute under pressure! The working out what he wants has been about as difficult as I reckoned; just keeping trying stuff until you hit on something that works! Hearing him crying is the most awful thing in the world, the amount of times I ended up in tears as well in the first few weeks and still sometimes do. Getting out of the house is neither the complete mission I anticipated or as easy as I thought. I can be ready to go out the door in 5 minutes easy but getting to an appointment at a particular time takes about 2 hours of planning to get the naps/feeds in the right place. 

One of the things that I was really worried about was the sleep and all the mothers I asked just said 'oh, you just get used to it' and I was like 'BUT HOW??????' And was really frustrated they couldn't explain. Now I'm here and I get it, its inexplicable that you all of a sudden can function on 5/6 hours broken up sleep a night whereas previously you were dead on your feet without a solid 7 but here we are! I think pregnancy prepares you for this in a big way; I think the last time I actually slept through the night was around April anyway. 

I've bounced around a bit in my summation of the first 3 months here; probably an accurate representation of my attention span and brain power at the moment. The one thing I'm happiest about? I was so worried about post-natal depression and bonding and we're not out of the woods yet on that one I know. But Day 2 in the hospital I looked down at my little boy and just thought ' yup, I'd definitely push Rob and anyone else I know under a bus for you'. The concerns I had about bonding with Bertie just didn't materialise and I am hugely grateful for that; it has made the first 3 months so much easier than it could have been and I'm looking forward to the next. 

 

Oh and the writing style? Yes it is an accurate reflection of how long it's taken me to write this, Bertie occasionally decides sleep is for the weak, usually after 3 days or so of really good napping to lull me into a false sense of security.

A month of Bertie

20/08/2016: Rob

As I write this I am currently sat on the sofa with a sleepy Bertie on my chest whilst Hannah gets some sleep. This has become a bit of a routine for us (me giving Bertie his last feed of the day) - or at least as close to one as we have at the moment!

It's a boy

On the 20 July 2016 we finally got to share with all our friends and family the news that our little one was a boy. Not sure how we managed to keep it from everyone for 21 weeks!

We had names picked for a long time, both for a boy and a girl, way before we were even really thinking of having a little one - even so it is hard to decide "that is the one". Thankfully he seems to have settled nicely into the name Bertie!

Might try to write a separate post about Bertie's name.

The first week

Bertie spent the best part of his first week in hospital whilst he and Hannah had a course of antibiotics. It also meant he spent this time with a cannula in his hand and a sock over it, which made leaning to change him a bit more difficult!

They both left hospital on the 26 July and we did the standard scary drive home - at one point using my phone camera to make sure he was ok as we couldn't see him in his seat.

What does he want?

The last few weeks, and I am guessing probably for the next year at least, we have been playing the 'what does Bertie want' game. You would think it would be pretty easy given the fairly small list of needs in his life, but that would be wrong and he lets us know when something is not right in his world!

Interspersed with the blur of naps, feeds and nappies has been lots of lovely visits and deliveries of presents and cards! Thanks to everyone we haven't had a chance to in person yet.

Bertie seems to delight in making us worry and his preference for causing us to jump out of bed is a cacophony of weird and wonderful noises a night - most of which sound like he is choking. Not good on a sleep addled brain at 3am...

23:18 20/08/2016

So the time is ticking around to Bertie being exactly a month old and as this is when I am planning to post this I will wrap this ramble up. Despite the weird noises Bertie is doing well and growing all the time. And despite the lack of sleep I love having this little cutie around. :-)

Do fancy a few beers though...


The first post - this website

25/06/2016: Rob

This website was hand crafted by me as a way to share pictures and video with friends and family as Hannah was uncomfortable with the idea of these being shared via social media.

Whilst professionally I work making websites I leave the coding to the experts, so for me it was an opportunity to try and see what I could actually do myself.

The geeky(ish) bit

This site was written from scratch by me (with some help in places from colleagues) on my Chromebook using Codenvy , a cloud based IDE. The code is stored in github and the site is hosted on github pages.

The picture and video pages use the github API to populate the page with all the image/videos in a particular directory. I explored using a more secure storage facility for the content but getting this working exceeded what I was able to achieve with my setup.

I was keen to add some security to the site, but wanted to make this proportional. Currently, all of the content and code is publicly available to anyone who was inclined to dig into my github account. However to add some barriers to accessing the site I added an encrypted homepage which upon entering the password redirects you to the actual content. If you can guess these URLs or bookmark them, you can get to the site itself without this password, but this page stops easy access.

As an extra small safeguard the site requests that it not be indexed by search engines.

Coding on a Chromebook

As I was writing this I was reflecting on how I had found coding on my Chromebook and decided to add my thoughts here:

For the most part this worked well and I am really pleased with how the site has turned out. With that said there were definite limitations with the combination of trying to learn a lot of this for the first time and working in the cloud. So many of the guides, answers to questions and helpful information out there is not targeted at people working in this way and it means that there is an additional layer of complexity to anything you try to do.

There were a number of occasions where I reached dead ends and seemingly simple things became extremely difficult and quickly exceeded my ability and knowledge. I am sure that anyone with more experience could have worked round many of these issues and I think that if I look to do more in the future I will need to look to have a setup that is much closer to what everyone expects beginners to use!
